# Stakeholder Analysis
## Process
### Step 1: Identify Stakeholders
| Stakeholder | Role | Organization | Relationship to Project |
|------------|------|-------------|----------------------|
| [name] | [title] | [dept/company] | Sponsor/Contributor/Affected/Influencer |
### Step 2: Map Influence and Interest
| Quadrant | Strategy |
|----------|---------|
| High influence, High interest | **Manage closely** — key players |
| High influence, Low interest | **Keep satisfied** — engage on their terms |
| Low influence, High interest | **Keep informed** — enthusiastic supporters |
| Low influence, Low interest | **Monitor** — minimal effort |
### Step 3: Assess Each Stakeholder
| Stakeholder | Influence (1-5) | Interest (1-5) | Sentiment | Engagement Need |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|-----------|----------------|
| [name] | X | X | Supportive/Neutral/Resistant | [strategy] |

| Stakeholder | Communication | Frequency | Channel | Owner |
|------------|--------------|-----------|---------|-------|
| [name] | [what to share] | [how often] | [email/meeting/report] | [PM] |
### Step 5: Manage Resistance
| Resistant Stakeholder | Concern | Mitigation |
|---------------------|---------|-----------|
| [name] | [what they worry about] | [how to address] |
## Output Format
```markdown
## Stakeholder Analysis: [Project]
### Map: [Influence-Interest grid]
### Engagement Plan: [Per stakeholder]
### Risks: [Resistant stakeholders with mitigations]
```
## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All stakeholders identified (including indirect)
- [ ] Influence and interest assessed honestly
- [ ] Engagement plan matches stakeholder needs
- [ ] Resistance management planned proactively
- [ ] Plan reviewed and updated regularly
## Edge Cases
- For large programs, group minor stakeholders into categories
- If political dynamics are complex, map informal influence separately
- For external stakeholders, engagement may need legal/comms review
